Goal

The goal was to create a unique logo and sticker pack, the final designs were used to produce merchandise for Amazon employees.

Discovery

During our meeting, I discovered that the logo was going to be used for an upcoming development sprint with a focus on “innovation.” The client gravitated toward the imagery of a lightbulb to signify innovation and the ideas that the team will bring to the table during this sprint.

Logo & Toque

My first step was a series of logo roughs produced with Procreate on the iPad. During this stage, I played with various elements you might find in code, along with shapes of lightbulbs and text layouts.





After the roughs were produced Illustrator was used to iterate on the designs to produce a final product. Leveraging Amazon’s brand identity l used the Amazon Ember font and foundation colours (Squid Ink, and Smile Orange.)





The client requested that we change the ASBX organization logo to focus on the specific ‘S4O’ team name. The text was adapted to put focus on the individual team while using some creative licensing to maintain the ‘X’ component of the ASBX logo within the design.



The final design was sent and then approved by the client. The logo was used to produce team swag in the form of a slouchy toque.



COE Sticker Designs

In addition to the S40 logo, the manager requested additional image assets that would be used to produce a series of stickers featuring Amazon’s retrospective process called a COE (Correction of Errors) through a series of danger and warning signs that employees could use on their laptops, water bottles & whiteboards.

After the designs were finalized, they were sent to the client for final approval and ordered in multiple sizes and finishes through Sticker Mule. The final designs were distributed to over 50 employees across six ASBX teams.
