Goal

MaiTAIme came to be as a project for my second term Adobe Illustrator class, we were tasked to create the brand identity and package designs for a fictional beverage company.


Based on my love for all things Tikis I came up with Mai TAIme, a new tiki-inspired premium cocktail in a can. The final deliverables include two the final logo, various poster designs, product renders, and packaging designs.

Logo & Brand Development

For the logo, I wanted to base my design on a mug in my own collection, with tropical garnish elements not specific to any beverage. I also took inspiration from a book in my collection titled: Tiki – Modern Tropical Cocktails by Shannon Mustipher.



I started the process by sketching ideas on Procreate for iPad, then brought those sketches into Adobe Illustrator, where I traced and further developed the logo asset using the pen tool.



Once I was happy with the logo outline, I selected analogous colours to enhance the overall design and introduce a level of kitsch to the design one would expect from a tiki company

3-D Development

Before I could bring the brand into a final design, additional assets needed to be created. In this case, I based my design on Polynesian-inspired patterns by layering various shapes, lines strokes and textures.



I then leveraged Illustrator's 3-D render capabilities to create a beverage can, and highball glass by extruding a simple line shape into a 3-D object.


Once both 3-D objects had been rendered, I mapped my logo and texture onto the digital assets.



Adobe Dimensions was introduced into the Adobe Creative Cloud in 2017 and allows designers to easily mock 3-D objects leveraging a library of existing objects, textures and lighting. We learnt and utilized this software in our New Technologies class (third term.)



For this asset I was able to leverage a pre-created can shape along with a stock texture to create a can that would hold a new variant of the Mai Taime logo featuring the name of the company in vertical alignment akin to most beverage cans.

Poster Design

For a final project in our Illustrator class we were tasked to combine all the elements we’ve developed for our beverage company into a marketing poster. The background for the posted needed to be rendered to support the theme of the brand, showcasing a place one might enjoy the beverage.


What’s more tiki than cocktails on a beach?



For the second poster, I used an image I took several years ago at The Shameful Tiki Room in Vancouver, featuring one of the Waikiki Tiki Girls.


I brought the image into Photoshop and created a mask around the dancer, I used this mask to desaturate the image around the dancer, while keeping the dancer in full colour. I made this decision as to ensure that there would be emphasis on the product, but have enough visual interest on the page to draw the user in.
