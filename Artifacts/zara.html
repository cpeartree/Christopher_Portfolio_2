Introduction:

In today's digital age, a website is often the first point of contact between a brand and its customers. As such, ensuring that a website is user-friendly and meets the needs of its target audience is crucial for a business's success. In this context, user experience (UX) research plays a critical role in identifying areas for improvement and driving meaningful change.

This case study on Zara's website highlights the importance of taking a user-centred approach to design and using both quantitative and qualitative data to inform design decisions. By implementing improvements to the website's information architecture, breadcrumbs, and in-store availability checkers, the study demonstrates how UX research can lead to significant improvements in website usability and customer satisfaction.

Reflection:

I feel a sense of accomplishment and growth in my skills as a user experience researcher. This project allowed me to apply the theoretical concepts I learned in my studies to a real-world problem, and to see firsthand the impact that user testing and data analysis can have on improving a website's usability.

One of the key takeaways from this project was the importance of taking a user-centred approach to design. By starting with hypothesis development and screening potential test candidates, we were able to ensure that our improvements were informed by the needs and preferences of Zara's target audience. Through the testing process, we were able to collect both qualitative and quantitative data that helped us understand user behaviour and identify areas for improvement.

Overall, this project helped me to develop a deeper understanding of user-centric design and the importance of gathering and analyzing data to drive meaningful change. I feel confident in my ability to apply these skills to future projects and continue to improve my craft as a user experience researcher.
